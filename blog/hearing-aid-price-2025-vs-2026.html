Hearing Aid Prices 2025 vs 2026 - What Changed?

December 25, 2025Buying Guide5 min read

If you have been considering purchasing a hearing aid in Pakistan, you may be wondering how prices have changed between 2025 and 2026. Currency fluctuations, import duties, and manufacturer pricing all affect the final cost you pay. This guide breaks down what has changed and helps you understand the current hearing aid market in Pakistan.

Good News: Despite economic pressures, our clinic has worked to keep hearing aids as affordable as possible. Entry-level Signia hearing aids remain available from Rs. 35,000, and we continue to offer the widest range of price points in Pakistan to suit every budget.

Factors Affecting Hearing Aid Prices

Currency Exchange Rates

Since hearing aids are imported products, the PKR to USD and EUR exchange rate directly affects pricing. The Pakistani Rupee's value against major currencies has fluctuated significantly, impacting the landed cost of hearing aid devices.

Import Duties and Taxes

Government import duty policies on medical devices change periodically. Any increase in customs duties, regulatory fees, or sales tax on medical equipment gets reflected in retail prices.

Manufacturer Pricing

Signia periodically releases new models and adjusts pricing globally. Newer technology models may command higher prices, while older models may see price reductions as they are phased out.

Technology Improvements

Each new generation of hearing aids includes better processing chips, improved algorithms, and new features. You get more value per rupee in 2026 compared to 2025, even if the nominal price is similar.

Price Comparison by Category

BTE Hearing Aids

BTE hearing aids remain the most affordable category. Entry-level models like the Signia Prompt SP continue to offer excellent value. The price range for BTE devices spans from Rs. 35,000 for basic models to Rs. 295,000 for premium options with advanced features.

RIC Hearing Aids

RIC hearing aids sit in the mid-to-premium range. These discreet, feature-rich devices start from approximately Rs. 85,000 and can go up to Rs. 390,000 for top-tier models with Bluetooth and AI-powered processing.

CIC Hearing Aids

CIC (invisible) hearing aids are custom-made devices. Prices start from Rs. 35,000 for basic models and can reach Rs. 325,000 for premium invisible models with advanced features.

Rechargeable Hearing Aids

Rechargeable hearing aids represent the premium segment. These devices, starting from Rs. 585,000 per pair, eliminate battery costs and offer the latest technology including Bluetooth streaming and AI sound processing.

Value Tip: When comparing prices, look beyond the sticker price. Consider what is included: hearing test, fitting, programming, follow-up adjustments, warranty, and after-sales service. At our clinic, all of these are included with every purchase, adding significant value compared to dealers who charge separately for services.

How to Get the Best Value

  • Buy from authorized dealers: Genuine products with proper warranty protection and professional fitting
  • Consider your actual needs: The most expensive hearing aid is not always the best for your specific hearing loss. Our audiologist recommends the most appropriate, not the most expensive
  • Ask about older models: Previous generation models may offer excellent performance at reduced prices
  • Think long-term: A well-fitted quality hearing aid lasts 5-7 years. Divide the price by the years of use to see the daily cost
  • Check for pair pricing: Buying bilateral (both ears) often offers better value per device

Should You Wait or Buy Now?

Many people delay buying hearing aids hoping prices will drop. In reality, hearing aid prices tend to remain stable or increase slightly over time due to currency depreciation and inflation. More importantly, every month you wait with untreated hearing loss is a month of reduced quality of life and potential cognitive decline. The best time to address hearing loss is always now.
