Traveling with Hearing Aids - Airport & Flight Tips

January 12, 2026Tips & Care5 min read

Whether you are flying from Islamabad International Airport for Hajj and Umrah, traveling to visit family in another city, or heading abroad for business or leisure, proper planning ensures your hearing aids perform perfectly throughout your journey. Many hearing aid users feel anxious about traveling, but with the right preparation, it is completely straightforward.

Travel presents unique hearing challenges: noisy airports, PA announcements you need to catch, communication with airline staff, and changing environments from city to city. Your hearing aids are your most important travel companion, and keeping them functioning properly throughout your trip is essential.

Key Fact: You can wear your hearing aids through airport security scanners without any issues. Hearing aids are not affected by metal detectors, X-ray machines, or body scanners. You do not need to remove them during security screening at any Pakistani or international airport.

Before Your Trip

Pre-Travel Checklist

  • Hearing aid check-up: Visit our clinic for a cleaning and performance check before any major trip. We will ensure everything is working perfectly.
  • Extra batteries: Pack at least twice as many batteries as you think you will need. Specific hearing aid battery sizes may be difficult to find abroad or in remote areas of Pakistan.
  • Charger and adapter: For rechargeable hearing aids, pack your charger in your carry-on bag. If traveling internationally, bring a universal power adapter.
  • Cleaning supplies: Pack your cleaning brush, wax pick, and dry cloth. A portable dehumidifier is essential for humid destinations.
  • Protective case: Always have a hard protective case for storing hearing aids during water activities, sleeping, or when you need to remove them.

At the Airport

Security Screening

Keep your hearing aids in your ears during security screening. Metal detectors and body scanners are completely safe for hearing aids. If security personnel ask about them, simply explain that you are wearing hearing aids. Airport staff at Islamabad, Lahore, Karachi, and international airports are familiar with hearing aids.

Catching Announcements

Airport PA systems can be challenging for hearing aid users due to echo and background noise. Here are strategies that help:

  • Sit near the departure gate screens and monitor flight information visually
  • Enable flight notifications on your phone's airline app for gate changes and boarding calls
  • If you have Bluetooth hearing aids, stream your phone's audio to stay informed
  • Inform gate staff about your hearing loss so they can alert you personally when boarding begins

During the Flight

Cabin Pressure

Changes in cabin pressure during takeoff and landing do not damage hearing aids. However, some users report slight discomfort if wearing tight-fitting ear molds. If you experience this, yawning or swallowing helps equalize pressure, just as it does for passengers without hearing aids.

In-Flight Entertainment

If you have Bluetooth hearing aids, you can stream movies and music from your phone or tablet directly to your hearing aids during the flight. For the aircraft entertainment system, use the provided headphones over your hearing aids or switch to a phone program for clarity.

Communication with Crew

Inform the cabin crew about your hearing loss during boarding. Flight attendants are trained to accommodate passengers with hearing impairment and can provide important announcements directly to you.

At Your Destination

  • Climate adjustment: If traveling from Islamabad to a humid coastal city or a hot desert destination, follow extra moisture protection measures for your hearing aids.
  • Hotel safety: Use the hotel room safe for storing spare hearing aids or expensive accessories. Keep your dehumidifier at your bedside for nightly use.
  • Water activities: Remove hearing aids before swimming, snorkeling, or beach activities. Store them in your waterproof case in a cool, shaded place.
  • Local emergency numbers: Save local emergency numbers in your phone and ensure your hearing aids are charged before exploring unfamiliar areas.
