Hearing Aids for Phone Calls - Bluetooth Streaming Guide

February 3, 2026 Technology 5 min read

Phone calls remain essential for staying connected with family, conducting business, and managing daily affairs in Pakistan. But for people with hearing loss, phone conversations can be one of the most challenging situations. You cannot see the caller's face for lip-reading cues, and phone audio quality is often compressed and tinny. Bluetooth hearing aids transform this experience by streaming phone calls directly to both ears with crystal-clear clarity.

Traditional phone use with hearing aids was problematic. Holding a phone near a hearing aid often caused whistling feedback, and the sound only reached one ear. Modern Bluetooth hearing aids eliminate both problems by wirelessly receiving the call audio and delivering it to both ears simultaneously, much like premium wireless earbuds but with hearing aid amplification tailored to your audiogram.

Both Ears, One Phone: When you stream a phone call to Bluetooth hearing aids, the caller's voice is delivered to both ears at the same time. This binaural listening significantly improves speech understanding compared to holding a phone to just one ear. Many of our patients say phone calls become effortless for the first time in years.

How Bluetooth Hearing Aid Phone Streaming Works

Direct Bluetooth Connection

Signia's latest hearing aids use Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE) technology to connect directly to your smartphone. Once paired, incoming calls are automatically routed to your hearing aids. You hear the caller in both ears, and the hearing aid's built-in microphone picks up your voice so you can speak naturally without holding the phone.

Compatible Phones

Bluetooth hearing aids work with most modern smartphones available in Pakistan, including iPhone (iOS) and Android devices from Samsung, Oppo, Vivo, Xiaomi, and other popular brands. Our audiologist will verify compatibility with your specific phone during the fitting appointment.

Hands-Free Operation

With Bluetooth streaming, you can answer calls with a tap on your hearing aid and speak hands-free. This is particularly useful while driving, cooking, or carrying items. Your phone can stay in your pocket or on a table across the room.

Non-Bluetooth Options

If you prefer simpler technology or have a budget constraint, our BTE hearing aids and CIC hearing aids can still improve phone calls significantly. Simply switch to the phone program and hold the phone near the hearing aid microphone. While not as seamless as Bluetooth, properly programmed hearing aids make phone conversations much clearer.

WhatsApp and Video Call Compatibility

In Pakistan, WhatsApp is the primary communication platform for many people. Bluetooth hearing aids work seamlessly with WhatsApp voice calls, video calls, and voice messages. The audio streams directly to your hearing aids just like a regular phone call. This also applies to other popular apps like Zoom, Skype, and FaceTime.

Video calls have an added advantage because you can see the caller's face on screen while hearing their voice clearly through both hearing aids, combining visual cues with amplified audio for maximum understanding.

Tips for Better Phone Calls with Hearing Aids

  • Find a Quiet Spot: Even with Bluetooth streaming, taking important calls in a quiet room improves clarity significantly.
  • Keep Your Phone Updated: Bluetooth connectivity works best with the latest software on your phone. Keep your phone's operating system updated.
  • Charge Your Hearing Aids: Bluetooth streaming uses more battery. Ensure your hearing aids are well charged before important calls.
  • Use the Signia App: The Signia smartphone app lets you adjust volume, treble, and bass during calls for personalized clarity.
  • Practice the Controls: Learn how to answer, end, and adjust volume on your hearing aids so the process becomes second nature.
