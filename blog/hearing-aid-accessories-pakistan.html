Essential Hearing Aid Accessories Available in Pakistan

January 1, 2026 Tips & Care 6 min read

Owning a hearing aid is just the beginning. To get the best performance and longest life from your device, you need the right accessories. From batteries and cleaning tools to connectivity devices and protective cases, this guide covers every essential accessory available in Pakistan for hearing aid users.

Good News: All the accessories mentioned in this guide are available at our Islamabad clinic and can be shipped anywhere in Pakistan. We stock genuine Signia accessories as well as compatible products from trusted brands.

Batteries

For non-rechargeable hearing aids, batteries are the most frequently purchased accessory. Hearing aid batteries come in four standard sizes, each with a color-coded tab:

  • Size 10 (Yellow): Used in small CIC and IIC hearing aids. Lasts 3-5 days.
  • Size 312 (Brown): Used in most RIC and small BTE hearing aids. Lasts 5-7 days.
  • Size 13 (Orange): Used in standard BTE hearing aids. Lasts 7-10 days.
  • Size 675 (Blue): Used in powerful Super Power and Ultra Power BTE hearing aids. Lasts 10-14 days.

We recommend using branded zinc-air batteries from reputable manufacturers. Cheap, no-name batteries may leak, corrode battery contacts, or provide inconsistent power.

Drying and Dehumidifying Accessories

Pakistan's humid climate makes moisture management critical for hearing aid longevity:

Drying Jar with Desiccant

A simple, affordable option. Place your hearing aid in the jar overnight with a desiccant capsule that absorbs moisture. The desiccant should be replaced monthly or when it changes color. This is the minimum moisture protection every hearing aid user in Pakistan should have.

Electronic Drying Box

A more effective solution that uses gentle heat and air circulation to dry hearing aids. Some models also use UV light to sanitize the device. These are particularly recommended for users in Islamabad's humid monsoon season or for people who sweat heavily.

Cleaning Accessories

Cleaning Brush

A small brush with soft bristles for daily cleaning of the microphone port and sound outlet. Most hearing aids come with one, and replacements are readily available.

Wax Pick and Wire Loop

Used to gently remove earwax from the sound outlet and vent openings. Essential for preventing wax-related blockages that reduce sound quality.

Wax Guards and Filters

Disposable filters that sit in the sound outlet to prevent wax from entering the receiver. These should be replaced every 1-2 months or when sound quality decreases.

Cleaning Spray and Wipes

Specially formulated cleaning sprays and wipes designed for hearing aids. Never use water, alcohol, or household cleaners on your hearing aid.

Daily Cleaning Routine: Wipe your hearing aid with a dry cloth every evening. Brush the microphone port and sound outlet. Open the battery door overnight to let moisture escape. This simple routine can prevent most common hearing aid problems.

Replacement Parts

  • Domes: Silicone tips for RIC hearing aids, available in open, closed, and power styles. Replace every 2-3 months.
  • Tubes: Plastic tubing connecting BTE hearing aids to ear molds. Replace every 3-6 months or when they become stiff or discolored.
  • Ear hooks: The curved piece connecting the BTE hearing aid body to the tube. Replace if cracked.
  • Receivers: The speaker component in RIC hearing aids. Replace if sound becomes distorted or weak.

Connectivity Accessories

For Signia hearing aids with wireless capability, several connectivity accessories enhance functionality:

  • StreamLine TV: Streams television audio directly to your hearing aids wirelessly
  • StreamLine Mic: A remote microphone that someone can clip on, streaming their voice directly to your hearing aids from across the room
  • Signia App: Free smartphone app for adjusting volume, changing programs, and requesting remote fine-tuning from your audiologist

Protective Accessories

  • Hard carrying case: Protects your hearing aid during travel
  • Retention clips: Cord clips that prevent hearing aids from falling and getting lost, especially useful for children and elderly users
  • Hearing aid sleeves: Soft covers that add grip and a layer of protection against sweat
