Signia Motion vs Pure - BTE or RIC? Which to Choose

March 3, 2026 Buying Guide 6 min read

The Signia Motion (BTE) and Signia Pure (RIC) are two different hearing aid styles that use the same processing technology but deliver it in fundamentally different packages. Choosing between them is one of the most common decisions our customers face. This guide explains the real differences so you can make an informed choice.

Quick Answer: Choose Motion BTE if you have severe hearing loss, prefer durability, or want longer battery life. Choose Pure RIC if you want maximum discretion, more natural sound quality, or have mild to moderate hearing loss.

Understanding the Styles

Motion (BTE - Behind-the-Ear)

The Motion BTE houses all components, including the speaker, behind the ear. Sound travels through a tube and ear hook to an ear mold in the ear canal. It is the traditional and most robust hearing aid style.

Pure (RIC - Receiver-in-Canal)

The Pure RIC places only the speaker (receiver) inside the ear canal, connected by a thin wire to the main unit behind the ear. The behind-the-ear unit is much smaller because it does not contain the speaker.

Size and Visibility

This is often the deciding factor. The Pure RIC is significantly smaller and more discreet than the Motion BTE. The thin wire connecting to the ear is nearly invisible, and the behind-the-ear unit can hide completely behind most ears. The Motion BTE is larger and more visible, with a noticeable ear hook and tubing.

Winner: Pure RIC for discretion. If appearance matters to you, the Pure is clearly more discreet.

Sound Quality

At the same technology tier (1Nx, 5Nx, 7Nx), both Motion and Pure share identical processing chips. However, the RIC design delivers sound differently, and many users find RIC sound more natural because the speaker is closer to the eardrum and the open fitting allows natural low-frequency sounds to enter the ear canal normally.

Winner: Pure RIC for natural sound quality, especially for mild to moderate hearing loss with open fittings.

Power and Hearing Loss Range

The Motion BTE has a significant advantage for severe and profound hearing loss. Its larger speaker behind the ear can produce more amplification than the tiny RIC receiver. The Motion SP (Super Power) can deliver up to 143 dB SPL, making it suitable for profound hearing loss that RIC devices cannot adequately address.

Winner: Motion BTE for severe to profound hearing loss. The Motion 7Nx SP is unmatched for powerful amplification.

Durability and Maintenance

BTE hearing aids are generally more durable because their speakers are protected inside the main housing behind the ear. RIC receivers, being inside the ear canal, are exposed to earwax and moisture, which can cause failures over time. Receiver replacements are straightforward and affordable, but BTE avoids this issue entirely.

Winner: Motion BTE for long-term durability and lower maintenance.

Battery Life

The Motion BTE uses Size 13 batteries lasting 10-14 days. The Pure RIC uses Size 312 batteries lasting 5-7 days. The BTE's larger battery compartment accommodates a bigger battery for longer life.

Winner: Motion BTE for battery life.

Comfort

This varies by individual. The Pure RIC's lighter weight and thin wire make it very comfortable for most users. The Motion BTE's ear hook and tubing can feel more secure but also more noticeable. For all-day wear, many users prefer the RIC's lighter feel.

Winner: Tie - depends on individual preference and ear anatomy.

Our Recommendation: For mild to moderate hearing loss, we typically recommend the Pure RIC for its natural sound and discreet design. For moderate to severe or profound hearing loss, the Motion BTE provides the power and durability needed. Visit our clinic for a hearing test to determine the best style for your specific hearing loss.

Comparison at Each Tier

  • Entry Level: Motion 1Nx vs Pure 1 - Both 16 channels. Choose based on hearing loss severity and cosmetic preference.
  • Advanced: Motion 5Nx vs Pure 5 - Both 32 channels with OVP. The Pure 5 is our most popular advanced hearing aid.
  • Premium: Motion 7Nx vs Pure 7 - Both 48 channels with Ultra HD e2e. The Motion 7Nx SP is best for severe loss; the Pure 7 is best for discretion.

Making Your Decision

The best approach is to visit our clinic where you can see and try both styles. Our audiologist will assess your hearing loss, examine your ears, and help you determine which style will give you the best results. Both the Motion and Pure are excellent hearing aids. The right choice depends on your specific hearing loss, lifestyle, and personal preferences.
