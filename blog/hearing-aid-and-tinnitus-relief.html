Using Hearing Aids for Tinnitus Relief - Sound Therapy

January 6, 2026Hearing Health5 min read

Tinnitus, the perception of ringing, buzzing, hissing, or whooshing sounds in the ears without any external source, affects millions of people in Pakistan. For many sufferers, tinnitus is a constant, distressing companion that interferes with sleep, concentration, work, and quality of life. While there is no cure for most forms of tinnitus, hearing aids have emerged as one of the most effective management tools, especially when tinnitus is accompanied by hearing loss.

Research shows that approximately 80% of tinnitus sufferers also have some degree of hearing loss. This connection is not coincidental. When the ear loses its ability to detect certain frequencies, the brain sometimes generates phantom sounds to fill the gap. By restoring those missing sounds with hearing aids, the brain has less reason to create tinnitus signals.

Understanding Tinnitus: Tinnitus is not a disease but a symptom. It can result from noise exposure, aging, ear infections, medications, stress, or underlying medical conditions. The first step is getting a comprehensive hearing evaluation to identify any hearing loss and determine the best management approach.

How Hearing Aids Help with Tinnitus

Sound Enrichment

The most basic way hearing aids help tinnitus is by amplifying environmental sounds. When you can hear the hum of air conditioning, distant traffic, bird songs, and conversation clearly, these natural sounds partially mask the tinnitus, making it less noticeable. Many tinnitus sufferers report that their ringing seems louder in quiet environments, so the sound enrichment provided by hearing aids is particularly effective.

Frequency-Specific Amplification

Since tinnitus often occurs at the same frequencies where hearing loss is greatest, restoring amplification at those specific frequencies can directly reduce the brain's tendency to generate phantom sounds. A well-programmed hearing aid targets exactly the frequencies where your hearing loss and tinnitus overlap.

Built-In Tinnitus Therapy

Signia hearing aids include dedicated tinnitus therapy programs that generate specially designed therapeutic sounds. These sounds can be customized to match and mask your specific tinnitus frequency and volume. Options include:

  • White Noise: A broad-spectrum sound that effectively masks most tinnitus types.
  • Pink Noise: Similar to white noise but with a warmer, less harsh character that many users prefer.
  • Ocean Waves: Gentle, rhythmic ocean sounds that provide soothing masking with a natural quality.
  • Notched Therapy: Advanced therapy sounds with a frequency notch at your tinnitus frequency, which trains the brain to suppress the tinnitus over time.

Living with Tinnitus in Pakistan

Tinnitus management is a multi-faceted approach. In addition to hearing aids, consider these strategies:

  • Stress management: Stress worsens tinnitus. Practice relaxation techniques, regular exercise, and adequate sleep.
  • Avoid silence: Keep a low-level background sound playing in quiet rooms, especially at bedtime. A small fan or soft music can help.
  • Protect your hearing: Avoid further noise exposure that could worsen both hearing loss and tinnitus. Use ear protection at weddings and loud events.
  • Limit caffeine and salt: Some people find that reducing caffeine and salt intake reduces tinnitus intensity.
  • Stay engaged: Social activities, hobbies, and staying mentally active help divert attention from tinnitus.
