Best Hearing Aid for Watching TV - Stream Directly

February 5, 2026 Tips & Care 5 min read

One of the most frustrating daily experiences for people with hearing loss is watching television. Whether it is following the news on ARY or Geo, watching a Pakistani drama, or enjoying a cricket match, unclear TV audio can turn an enjoyable activity into a source of tension, especially when family members complain about the volume being too loud. Modern hearing aids with TV streaming capability solve this problem completely.

The challenge with TV audio and hearing loss is twofold. First, TV speakers project sound in a general direction, and by the time it reaches your ears across the room, it has lost clarity. Second, background sounds in the room such as air conditioning, kitchen noise, or family conversations compete with the TV audio, making dialogue even harder to follow.

Family Harmony: TV volume disputes are one of the top reasons families come to our clinic. With streaming hearing aids, you hear the TV at your perfect volume directly in your ears while the TV stays at a comfortable level for everyone else in the room. No more arguments about the remote control.

How TV Streaming Works with Hearing Aids

TV streaming hearing aids receive audio wirelessly from your television through a small device called a TV Connector. Here is how the system works:

  • TV Connector Device: A small box plugs into your TV's audio output (headphone jack, optical, or HDMI ARC). It transmits audio wirelessly to your hearing aids.
  • Direct Streaming: The TV audio streams directly into both hearing aids simultaneously, delivering crystal-clear sound as if you were wearing premium headphones.
  • Adjustable Mix: You can blend the TV audio with environmental sounds, so you can still hear if someone speaks to you while watching TV.
  • Personal Volume: Adjust the TV streaming volume independently from the room's TV speakers. You hear at your level, others hear at theirs.

Setting Up Your TV Streaming System

Getting started with TV streaming is straightforward. Here is what you need to know:

Compatible TVs

The TV Connector works with virtually any television that has an audio output. This includes modern smart TVs, older LCD/LED TVs, and even cable/satellite set-top boxes. Whether you have a Samsung, LG, Sony, or any other brand, the system will work.

Installation

Setting up the TV Connector takes about five minutes. Plug it into your TV's audio output, connect the power cable, and pair it with your hearing aids. Our audiologist will handle the initial pairing during your fitting appointment, and we provide clear instructions for reconnecting if needed.

Range and Performance

The TV Connector streams audio up to 7 meters (about 23 feet) from the TV, which is more than enough for any typical Pakistani drawing room or lounge. The audio latency is virtually zero, meaning the sound in your hearing aids stays perfectly synchronized with the picture on screen.

Multiple Viewers: The TV Connector can stream to an unlimited number of compatible hearing aids simultaneously. If both husband and wife wear Signia hearing aids, both can stream TV audio at their individual preferred volumes from the same TV Connector device.

Tips for Better TV Listening

  • Use Subtitles Too: Combining hearing aids with Urdu or English subtitles provides the best comprehension, especially for dramas with soft dialogue.
  • Optimize TV Sound Settings: Many TVs have a "clear voice" or "dialogue enhancement" mode in their sound settings. Enable this for clearer speech output.
  • Reduce Room Noise: Turn off fans or AC units that create background noise during important programs.
  • Position Your Seating: Even with hearing aids, sitting directly facing the TV at a moderate distance gives the best results.
