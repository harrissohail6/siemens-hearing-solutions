Hearing Aids for Retired Army & Military Personnel Pakistan

March 20, 2026 Buying Guide 5 min read

Pakistan's armed forces personnel dedicate decades of their lives to protecting the nation. During their service, they are exposed to extreme noise levels from gunfire, artillery, armored vehicles, aircraft engines, and explosions. This prolonged exposure to high-decibel noise takes a serious toll on hearing, often becoming apparent only after retirement when the damage has already been done.

If you are a retired army officer, JCO, soldier, or veteran from any branch of Pakistan's armed forces, this guide is for you.

Did You Know? Military personnel are exposed to noise levels of 140-185 dB during weapons firing and explosions. Hearing damage begins at just 85 dB. Many retired military members have significant noise-induced hearing loss (NIHL) that hearing aids can effectively address.

Why Military Service Causes Hearing Loss

Noise-induced hearing loss (NIHL) is the most common service-related health condition among military veterans worldwide. In Pakistan's armed forces, common noise sources include:

  • Small arms fire: A standard rifle produces 150-165 dB per shot. Even with hearing protection, repeated exposure over years causes cumulative damage.
  • Artillery and mortars: Artillery pieces generate 180+ dB at the operator's position. Artillery crews are among the most affected.
  • Armored vehicles: Tank crews and APC operators are exposed to constant engine and track noise of 100-115 dB during operations.
  • Helicopters and aircraft: Army Aviation and PAF personnel face 95-140 dB from helicopter and jet engines.
  • Improvised explosive devices (IEDs): Blast exposure can cause both immediate and delayed hearing damage.

The Pattern of Military Hearing Loss

Military hearing loss typically shows a distinctive pattern on an audiogram known as a "noise notch" - a sharp dip in hearing at 4,000 Hz with better hearing at lower and higher frequencies. This pattern affects speech clarity significantly because many consonant sounds (s, f, th, sh) fall in this frequency range.

Hearing Loss and Post-Service Life

Untreated hearing loss after military service affects every aspect of civilian life. Veterans report difficulty in family conversations, social gatherings, watching television, using phones, and attending mosque. Many veterans withdraw from social activities, leading to isolation and depression.

A properly fitted hearing aid can reverse these effects dramatically. Our veteran patients consistently tell us that getting a hearing aid was one of the best decisions they made after retirement. It reconnected them with their families and communities.
