Why Authorized Matters for Your Hearing

Hearing aids are medical devices, not consumer electronics. Buying from an unauthorized source carries significant risks that can affect your hearing health and financial investment:

Risk of Counterfeit Products

Unauthorized sellers may offer hearing aids at suspiciously low prices. These are often counterfeit devices or refurbished units stripped of their original warranty. A counterfeit hearing aid may amplify sound but lacks the sophisticated processing algorithms that protect your hearing from further damage.

No Professional Fitting

Without access to Connexx software, unauthorized sellers cannot properly program a Signia hearing aid to your unique audiogram. An improperly fitted hearing aid can under-amplify (making it ineffective) or over-amplify (potentially causing further hearing damage). Our clinic ensures every device is fitted based on proper hearing test results.

No Warranty Coverage

Signia's manufacturer warranty only applies to devices purchased through authorized channels. If your hearing aid develops a fault and was bought from an unauthorized seller, Signia will not honor the warranty, leaving you to pay for expensive repairs out of pocket.

No After-Sales Support

Hearing aids require periodic adjustments as your hearing changes over time. Authorized dealers provide this ongoing support, typically at no extra charge. Unauthorized sellers usually disappear after the sale.

Verify Before You Buy: When purchasing any Signia hearing aid, ask the seller for the device serial number and verify it with Signia. You can also ask to see the Connexx software being used for fitting. If the seller cannot provide these, they are likely not authorized.
