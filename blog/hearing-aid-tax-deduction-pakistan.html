Hearing Aid Tax Benefits in Pakistan - Disability Exemptions

February 13, 2026 Buying Guide 5 min read

Many hearing aid users in Pakistan are unaware that they may be eligible for certain tax benefits and exemptions related to their disability. The Government of Pakistan provides several provisions for persons with disabilities, including those with hearing impairment. This guide explains the potential tax advantages and how to access them.

Disability Certificate for Hearing Impairment

To access any disability-related benefits in Pakistan, you first need to obtain a disability certificate from the relevant provincial authority:

  • Punjab: Department of Social Welfare, or through NADRA special registration
  • Sindh: Directorate General of Special Education
  • KPK: Social Welfare Department
  • Federal: Directorate General of Special Education, Islamabad

A person with significant hearing loss (typically 40dB or more in both ears) may qualify for a disability certificate. You will need an audiogram from a certified audiologist and a medical certificate from an ENT specialist.

Income Tax Benefits

Reduced Tax Rate for Disabled Persons

Under the Income Tax Ordinance 2001, persons with disabilities who hold a valid CNIC with disability status may be eligible for a reduced tax rate. The specific benefits can vary by tax year, so it is important to check the latest Finance Act provisions.

Medical Expense Deductions

Hearing aid purchases may qualify as medical expenses that can be claimed against your taxable income. Keep all invoices and medical prescriptions as documentation. Our invoices include our NTN number for your records.

Customs Duty Exemptions

Hearing aids imported for personal use by a person with hearing disability may be eligible for customs duty exemption under SRO notifications. This primarily applies when importing hearing aids directly, though most customers purchase locally from authorized dealers like us.

Employment Quota Benefits

The Disabled Persons Employment and Rehabilitation Ordinance 1981 requires organizations with more than 100 employees to reserve a percentage of jobs for persons with disabilities. A disability certificate for hearing impairment can help qualify for these reserved positions.

How to Maximize Your Benefits

  • Get Your Disability Certificate: If your hearing loss qualifies, obtain a disability certificate from the relevant authority.
  • Update Your CNIC: Register your disability status with NADRA on your CNIC.
  • Keep All Receipts: Maintain invoices for hearing aids, batteries, and accessories for tax purposes.
  • Consult a Tax Professional: A tax advisor can help you identify all applicable deductions and exemptions.
