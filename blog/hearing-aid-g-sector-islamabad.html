Hearing Aid in G-Sectors Islamabad - G-6, G-8, G-9, G-10, G-11

March 30, 2026 Location Guide 5 min read

The G-sectors of Islamabad stretch from the centrally located G-6, home to the famous Melody Market and Aabpara Market, all the way to G-13 near the Golra interchange. If you live anywhere in this corridor, our authorized Signia hearing aid center in Blue Area is your closest professional hearing clinic. This guide explains how to reach us, what services we offer, and which hearing aids suit different needs and budgets.

Getting to Our Center from the G-Sectors

Blue Area runs parallel to the G-6 and G-7 sectors, separated only by Jinnah Avenue. This means G-sector residents have an incredibly convenient location for hearing healthcare. Here is how long it takes from each area:

  • G-6 (Melody Market / Aabpara): 3-5 minutes by car. You can literally cross Jinnah Avenue and you are in Blue Area. Aabpara residents can walk to our center in about 10 minutes.
  • G-7 (Sitara Market area): 5-7 minutes. Head south on any connecting road to reach Jinnah Avenue, then a short drive east to our location.
  • G-8 (G-8 Markaz): 7-10 minutes. Take the Khayaban-e-Suhrwardy road towards the Convention Center, then continue to Blue Area.
  • G-9 (Karachi Company): 8-12 minutes. The Karachi Company bus stop is a well-known landmark. From there, head east on Khayaban-e-Suhrwardy towards the Secretariat and Blue Area.
  • G-10 and G-11: 12-15 minutes via Khayaban-e-Suhrwardy or through the IJP Road connector. Straightforward route with minimal traffic signals.
  • G-13 (Golra side): 18-22 minutes via Kashmir Highway. Take the Faisal Avenue exit and continue to Blue Area.
Public Transport: G-sector residents can take the Metro Bus or local wagons from Karachi Company or Aabpara to Secretariat, which is walking distance from our Blue Area clinic. This makes us accessible even without a personal vehicle.

Why G-Sector Residents Need Professional Hearing Care

The G-sectors have a diverse population, from long-time government employees in G-6 and G-7 to younger families in G-10 and G-11. Many of our patients from these areas are retired civil servants, military officers, or professionals who first notice hearing difficulties during social gatherings at places like the Islamabad Club or community events.

Unlike the small shops in Melody Market or Aabpara that sell cheap amplifiers, our center provides clinical-grade hearing solutions with proper testing, fitting, and ongoing support. The difference between a Rs. 5,000 amplifier from Aabpara and a Rs. 35,000 Signia hearing aid is the difference between muffled noise and clear, natural sound.
