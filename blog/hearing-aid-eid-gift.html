Gift a Hearing Aid This Eid - Best Gift for Parents & Elders

March 26, 2026 Buying Guide 5 min read

Every Eid, we search for the perfect gift for our parents and elders. We buy clothes, perfumes, sweets, and give Eidi envelopes. But what if this Eid you could give a gift that truly changes their life? A hearing aid is not just a device - it is the gift of connection, conversation, and participation in family celebrations.

If your parent, grandparent, or elder relative has been struggling with hearing loss, gifting them a hearing aid this Eid could be the most meaningful and rewarding gift you ever give.

Most Meaningful Eidi: A hearing aid lets your parents hear their grandchildren's voices, participate in Eid family conversations, listen to the Eid khutbah clearly, and feel included in celebrations. It is a gift that keeps giving every single day.

Why a Hearing Aid Makes the Perfect Eid Gift

Eid is about family, togetherness, and celebration. For someone with hearing loss, these joyful occasions can feel isolating. They sit quietly while everyone laughs and talks, unable to follow conversations. They smile and nod without really hearing what is being said. This is not how anyone should experience Eid.

Signs Your Parent May Need a Hearing Aid

  • They keep the TV volume very high
  • They frequently ask "what?" or "say that again?"
  • They seem withdrawn or quiet at family gatherings
  • They complain that people "mumble"
  • They have difficulty hearing on the phone
  • They cannot hear the doorbell or phone ringing
  • They struggle to follow conversations in noisy environments

If you notice any of these signs, your parent or elder likely has hearing loss that a hearing aid can address.

Sadaqah Jariyah - A Continuing Charity

In Islam, sadaqah jariyah is a form of ongoing charity that continues to benefit someone long after the initial act. A hearing aid can be seen as a form of sadaqah jariyah for your family. By helping your parent hear the Quran, attend prayers, learn, and communicate, you are enabling acts of worship that generate ongoing rewards. The benefit continues every single day for years.

What If They Are Reluctant?

Many elders resist getting hearing aids due to pride, stigma, or the belief that hearing loss is "just part of aging." Here is how to approach this gently:

  • Frame it as health care: "Ammi/Abbu, this is for your health, just like your blood pressure medicine."
  • Show modern designs: Today's hearing aids are tiny and nearly invisible. Show them CIC models that sit completely inside the ear canal.
  • Emphasize family connection: "We want to talk to you and have you be part of our conversations."
  • Mention religious benefits: "You'll be able to hear the khutbah and Quran recitation clearly."
