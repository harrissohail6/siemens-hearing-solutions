Can You Buy Hearing Aids on Installment in Pakistan?

January 2, 2026 Buying Guide 5 min read

One of the biggest barriers to getting a hearing aid in Pakistan is cost. Quality hearing aids from established brands like Signia can range from PKR 35,000 to over PKR 350,000 per device. For many families, paying the full amount upfront is simply not possible. The good news is that installment plans and flexible payment options can make hearing aids accessible to everyone.

The Affordability Challenge

Hearing aids are a medical necessity, not a luxury. Yet in Pakistan, there is no government subsidy or insurance coverage for hearing aids. This means the entire cost falls on the patient and their family. Consider these realities:

  • Many hearing loss patients are elderly and retired, living on pensions or family support
  • Some patients need two hearing aids (one for each ear), doubling the cost
  • Families often have competing expenses like education, rent, and medical bills
  • The cost of hearing aids is not a one-time expense - batteries, maintenance, and eventual replacement add up

Because of these financial pressures, many people either delay getting hearing aids (which worsens their condition) or buy cheap, ineffective copies that break within months.

Why Investing in Hearing Aids Matters

Some people view hearing aids as an optional expense. Here is why they should be considered a priority investment:

Health Benefits

  • Cognitive health: Studies show that untreated hearing loss accelerates cognitive decline and increases the risk of dementia
  • Mental health: Hearing loss leads to social isolation, depression, and anxiety. Hearing aids reverse this.
  • Safety: Hearing aids help you detect warning sounds - traffic, alarms, and calls for help
  • Balance: Untreated hearing loss is linked to increased fall risk in the elderly

Social and Professional Benefits

  • Better relationships: Clear communication strengthens family bonds and friendships
  • Work performance: For working professionals, hearing clearly in meetings and conversations is essential
  • Independence: Seniors with hearing aids maintain their independence longer
  • Quality of life: Enjoying TV, music, conversations, and daily sounds enriches life immeasurably

Financial Perspective

When you break down the cost of a quality hearing aid over its 5-7 year lifespan, it often works out to less than PKR 50-100 per day. That is less than a cup of chai. For a device you wear 12-16 hours daily that dramatically improves your quality of life, it is an outstanding investment.

Beware of "Cheap" Alternatives

When money is tight, it is tempting to buy a cheap hearing amplifier or China copy hearing aid for PKR 5,000-15,000. But consider this:

  • Cheap devices are not programmed for your specific hearing loss - they just make everything louder, including noise
  • They often break within 3-6 months, wasting your money entirely
  • They can actually damage your remaining hearing due to uncontrolled amplification
  • There is no warranty, no repair option, no after-sales support
  • You end up spending more money replacing cheap devices than you would on one quality hearing aid

A genuine Signia hearing aid on installment is always a better choice than a cheap copy paid in full.
