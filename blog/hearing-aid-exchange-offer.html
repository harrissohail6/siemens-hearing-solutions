Why Upgrade Your Hearing Aid?

Technology Advances Rapidly

Hearing aid technology evolves faster than most consumer electronics. A device from 2020-2021 lacks features that are now standard in 2026 models, including advanced noise management algorithms, Bluetooth connectivity, rechargeable batteries, and artificial intelligence-driven sound processing.

Your Hearing May Have Changed

Hearing loss typically progresses over time. Your current hearing aid may no longer have enough power or the right frequency range to address your current hearing needs. A new device programmed to your current audiogram will provide noticeably better results.

Better Comfort and Fit

Newer models are smaller, lighter, and more comfortable. If your current device is bulky or uncomfortable, a modern RIC or CIC hearing aid will feel like a significant upgrade in daily wearability.

Reliability Concerns

Older hearing aids become less reliable over time. Components wear out, battery contacts corrode, and receivers weaken. If you are experiencing frequent breakdowns or reduced performance, upgrading is more cost-effective than repeated repairs.

Technology Comparison: A hearing aid from 2020 typically had 8-16 channels and basic noise reduction. Today's Signia devices offer up to 48 channels, advanced directional microphones, own voice processing, Bluetooth streaming, and rechargeable batteries lasting a full day. The improvement in daily hearing experience is substantial.

Signs It Is Time to Upgrade

Consider upgrading your hearing aid if:

  • Your device is over 5 years old: Technology has advanced significantly
  • You struggle in noisy places: Modern devices handle noise far better than older models
  • Frequent repairs: Multiple repairs suggest the device is reaching end of life
  • You want Bluetooth: Streaming phone calls, music, and TV directly to your hearing aids requires newer technology
  • Battery hassle: If changing tiny batteries is frustrating, rechargeable models eliminate this daily task
  • Hearing has worsened: Your current device may not have enough power for your current hearing loss level
