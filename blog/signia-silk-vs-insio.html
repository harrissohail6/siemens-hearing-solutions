Signia Silk vs Insio - Click Fit vs Custom Invisible

March 2, 2026 Buying Guide 6 min read

Both the Signia Silk and Signia Insio are invisible hearing aids that sit inside your ear canal. The difference is how they fit. The Silk uses click-fit silicone sleeves for instant fitting, while the Insio is custom-molded to your exact ear canal shape. This guide helps you decide which approach is better for your needs.

Quick Answer: Choose Silk for instant availability and convenience. Choose Insio for the best possible fit, comfort, and sound seal. Both deliver excellent invisible hearing at every technology tier.

Fitting Approach: The Fundamental Difference

Silk: Click-Fit Instant Fitting

The Silk comes with multiple silicone sleeve sizes. Our audiologist selects the right size, clicks it onto the device, and places it in your ear canal. The entire fitting takes minutes, and you walk out wearing your invisible hearing aids the same day.

Insio: Custom-Molded Precision

The Insio requires an ear impression using silicone material. This impression is sent to Signia's lab, where your hearing aid shell is built to match your ear canal exactly. This process takes additional time but produces the most precise fit possible.

Detailed Comparison

Fit Precision

The Insio wins here clearly. A custom mold matches every curve and contour of your ear canal, resulting in a fit that feels like it was made for you, because it was. The Silk's silicone sleeves are flexible and comfortable, but they use standardized sizes that approximate your ear canal shape rather than matching it exactly.

Comfort for Extended Wear

For wearing 10-16 hours daily, the Insio's custom fit typically provides superior long-term comfort. There are no pressure points because the shell matches your anatomy precisely. The Silk is comfortable for most users, but some people with unusual ear canal shapes may experience minor comfort issues with standardized sleeves.

Sound Seal and Quality

A better physical seal in the ear canal means better bass response and less sound leakage. The Insio's custom fit provides a more consistent seal, which can result in slightly better low-frequency reproduction. The Silk's sound quality is excellent, but the seal may be marginally less precise.

Convenience and Speed

The Silk wins decisively. You can be fitted and walking out with invisible hearing aids within an hour of arriving at our clinic. The Insio requires at least two visits: one for the ear impression and hearing test, and another for the fitting once the custom device is manufactured.

Replaceability

If you lose a Silk hearing aid, a replacement can be programmed and fitted immediately from stock. An Insio replacement requires new manufacturing. The Silk's click-fit sleeves are also easily replaceable if they wear out or get dirty.

Technology Tier Comparison

Both Silk and Insio are available at multiple technology tiers with the same processing power:

  • Mid-Range: Silk 3X vs Insio 3Nx - 24 channels, basic noise reduction
  • Advanced: Silk 5X vs Insio 5Nx - 32 channels with OVP
  • Premium: Silk 7X vs Insio 7Nx - 48 channels with Ultra HD e2e

At each tier, the Silk and Insio share identical processing capabilities. The only difference is the physical fitting approach.

Pro Tip: Some customers start with a Silk for immediate invisible hearing, then order a custom Insio as their primary device. They keep the Silk as a backup or travel device. This approach gives you the best of both worlds.

Who Should Choose Silk?

  • People who need invisible hearing immediately
  • First-time CIC users who want to try invisible hearing before committing to custom
  • Travelers who need hearing aids before a trip
  • People who value convenience and easy replacement
  • Users with standard-shaped ear canals that fit silicone sleeves well

Who Should Choose Insio?

  • Users who prioritize the absolute best fit and long-term comfort
  • People with unusual ear canal shapes that may not suit standard sleeves
  • Users who want the best possible sound seal and bass response
  • Long-term hearing aid users who know they want a permanent invisible solution
  • People who can wait for custom manufacturing
