CIC vs IIC Hearing Aids - Which is More Invisible?

February 20, 2026 Buying Guide 6 min read

If discretion is your top priority when choosing a hearing aid, you have likely come across two types: CIC (Completely-in-Canal) and IIC (Invisible-in-Canal). Both sit inside the ear canal and are designed to be as invisible as possible, but they differ in size, placement, and capabilities. This guide will help you understand which option is best for your needs.

Quick Answer: IIC hearing aids are slightly smaller and sit deeper in the ear canal, making them virtually undetectable. CIC hearing aids sit slightly higher but are still very discreet. Both are suitable for mild to moderate hearing loss. Our CIC models start from Rs. 35,000.

What is a CIC Hearing Aid?

A CIC (Completely-in-Canal) hearing aid is custom-molded to fit entirely within your ear canal. It sits at the opening of the ear canal, and while most of the device is hidden, a small extraction cord or faceplate may be visible to someone looking closely at your ear. CIC hearing aids are one of the smallest hearing aid types available.

What is an IIC Hearing Aid?

An IIC (Invisible-in-Canal) hearing aid goes even deeper into the ear canal than a CIC. It sits past the second bend of the ear canal, making it completely invisible from outside. Only a tiny extraction cord remains accessible for removal. IIC is the most discreet hearing aid type available today.

Key Differences

Visibility

  • CIC: Nearly invisible. A small faceplate may be slightly visible if someone looks directly into your ear.
  • IIC: Completely invisible from any angle. Even looking into the ear, the device is not visible.

Ear Canal Requirements

  • CIC: Suitable for most ear canal sizes. Can accommodate a wider range of ear shapes.
  • IIC: Requires a sufficiently wide and deep ear canal. Not suitable for very narrow or short ear canals.

Power and Amplification

  • CIC: Slightly more room for a larger speaker, allowing for moderate hearing loss coverage.
  • IIC: Smaller speaker due to compact size. Best for mild to moderate hearing loss only.

Battery Life

  • CIC: Uses size 10 batteries, lasting 3-7 days depending on usage.
  • IIC: Uses the smallest batteries (size 10), with shorter battery life of 3-5 days due to deeper placement.

Features

  • CIC: Can include volume control, program button (in some models), and wireless connectivity in premium versions.
  • IIC: Limited to automatic operation due to tiny size. No manual controls or Bluetooth in most models.
Which Should You Choose? If absolute invisibility is essential and your ear canal is large enough, IIC is the most discreet option. If you want a nearly invisible device with slightly more features and flexibility, CIC is the practical choice. Both are custom-made from an ear impression taken by an audiologist.

Who Should Consider CIC or IIC?

  • Professionals who attend meetings and want invisible hearing aids
  • People who are self-conscious about wearing hearing aids
  • Those with mild to moderate hearing loss
  • Users who prefer automatic operation without manual adjustments
  • People who do not need Bluetooth streaming features

Who Should Choose a Different Style?

CIC and IIC hearing aids are not suitable for everyone. If you have severe hearing loss, small ear canals, dexterity issues (difficulty handling tiny devices), or need Bluetooth connectivity, consider BTE or RIC hearing aids instead.
