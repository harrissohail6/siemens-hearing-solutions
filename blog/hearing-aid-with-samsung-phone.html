Using Signia Hearing Aids with Samsung Phones - Setup Guide

March 9, 2026Technology5 min read

Samsung Galaxy phones are the most popular smartphones in Pakistan, and they work very well with Signia Bluetooth hearing aids. This guide walks you through the complete setup process for connecting your Signia hearing aids with your Samsung Galaxy phone.

Compatibility: Signia Bluetooth hearing aids work with Samsung Galaxy S10 and later, Galaxy A52 and later, Galaxy Note 10 and later, and Galaxy Z Fold/Flip series. Your phone must run Android 10 or later for direct ASHA streaming.

Step-by-Step Pairing Guide

Step 1: Prepare Your Hearing Aids

Turn off your hearing aids by opening the battery door or placing rechargeable models in the charger, then turn them back on. They enter pairing mode automatically when freshly powered on.

Step 2: Enable Bluetooth

Go to Settings > Connections > Bluetooth and make sure Bluetooth is on.

Step 3: Pair via Accessibility

Go to Settings > Accessibility > Hearing Enhancements > Hearing Aids. Your phone will scan for hearing aids. Tap your Signia devices to pair. Accept any pairing requests.

Step 4: Install Signia App

Download the Signia app from Google Play Store. It will detect your already-paired hearing aids automatically.

Step 5: Test Streaming

Play music, YouTube, or make a phone call to verify audio streams to your hearing aids.

Troubleshooting: If your Samsung does not detect hearing aids: (1) Restart hearing aids. (2) Clear Bluetooth cache: Settings > Apps > Bluetooth > Storage > Clear Cache. (3) Restart your phone. (4) Ensure Android 10 or later.

What You Can Do After Pairing

  • Phone calls: Stream directly to both hearing aids. Talk naturally using the hearing aid microphone.
  • Media: YouTube, Spotify, Netflix stream to your hearing aids.
  • WhatsApp: Voice and video calls stream directly - perfect for family connections.
  • Navigation: Google Maps directions stream to hearing aids while driving.
  • Quran apps: Stream recitation privately through your hearing aids.

Samsung-Specific Features

  • Sound Amplifier: Settings > Accessibility > Hearing > Sound Amplifier uses phone mic to amplify nearby sounds to hearing aids
  • Adapt Sound: Settings > Sounds > Sound Quality > Adapt Sound creates custom audio profile
  • Flash notification: Camera flash blinks for calls when not wearing hearing aids

Battery Considerations

Bluetooth streaming uses more battery. Rechargeable hearing aids last 16-20 hours with moderate streaming. Battery-powered models may need changes 1-2 days sooner. Samsung phone battery drain increases about 10-15% per day with streaming active.
