Using Signia Hearing Aids with iPhone - Complete Guide

March 8, 2026Technology5 min read

Apple's iPhone offers the best smartphone experience for hearing aid users, thanks to the Made for iPhone (MFi) protocol. If you own an iPhone and Signia Bluetooth hearing aids, you get seamless audio streaming, powerful accessibility features, and effortless control.

Best Combo: iPhone + Signia Bluetooth hearing aids give you direct streaming for calls, music, FaceTime, and all apps. Plus exclusive Apple features like Live Listen (turns iPhone into a remote microphone) and native hearing aid controls in iOS.

Pairing Guide

Step 1: Update iOS

Ensure iOS 14 or later. Settings > General > Software Update.

Step 2: Prepare Hearing Aids

Turn off then back on. Signia MFi hearing aids enter pairing mode automatically when freshly powered on.

Step 3: Pair

Settings > Accessibility > Hearing Devices. Tap your Signia devices when they appear. Accept pairing for each ear.

Step 4: Signia App

Download from App Store for additional control, sound customization, and remote audiologist support.

Exclusive iPhone Features

Live Listen

Open Control Center, tap the ear icon. iPhone's microphone captures sound and streams it to your hearing aids in real-time. Place your iPhone near a speaker at a meeting and hear them clearly from across the room.

Siri Integration

Say "Hey Siri" and responses stream to your hearing aids. Make calls, send messages, get directions hands-free.

Quick Access

Triple-click your iPhone's side button to instantly open hearing aid controls. Set up in Settings > Accessibility > Accessibility Shortcut > Hearing Devices.

What You Can Stream

  • Phone calls and WhatsApp calls
  • Music from Apple Music, Spotify, YouTube Music
  • Video from YouTube, Netflix, and all apps
  • Quran recitation from Islamic apps
  • Navigation directions from Apple Maps and Google Maps
  • Podcasts and audiobooks
  • FaceTime video and audio calls
Battery Impact: With moderate streaming (2-3 hours daily), rechargeable Signia hearing aids last 16-20 hours. Battery models may last 1-2 days less with regular streaming. iPhone battery drain increases about 10-15% per day.

Troubleshooting

  • Connection drops: Toggle Bluetooth off and on. If persistent, unpair and re-pair.
  • Audio crackling: Move iPhone closer. Bluetooth range is about 10 meters.
  • Not found: Restart hearing aids and iPhone, then try again.
