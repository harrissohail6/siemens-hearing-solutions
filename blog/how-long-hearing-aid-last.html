How Long Does a Hearing Aid Last? Lifespan Guide

February 12, 2026 Tips & Care 5 min read

One of the most frequently asked questions about hearing aids is how long they last. The short answer is 4 to 6 years for most hearing aids, though some can last longer with excellent care. Understanding what affects hearing aid lifespan helps you get the most value from your investment and know when it is time for a replacement.

Average Lifespan: Most hearing aids last 4-6 years. BTE models tend to last longer (5-7 years) because their components are larger and more durable. CIC models may last 3-5 years due to their exposure to earwax and moisture inside the ear canal.

Factors That Affect Hearing Aid Lifespan

1. Type of Hearing Aid

BTE hearing aids generally last the longest because they sit behind the ear, away from moisture and earwax. CIC hearing aids sit inside the ear canal where they are exposed to more moisture and earwax, which can shorten their lifespan.

2. Maintenance and Cleaning

Regular cleaning is the single most important factor in extending hearing aid lifespan. Earwax buildup, dust, and moisture are the primary causes of hearing aid damage. Clean your hearing aid daily with a soft, dry cloth and use a wax pick to clear the sound outlet.

3. Climate and Environment

Pakistan's hot and humid climate can be challenging for hearing aids. Sweat, dust, and high humidity accelerate component wear. Using a dehumidifier box overnight can significantly extend your hearing aid's life.

4. Body Chemistry

Some people produce more earwax or have more acidic sweat, which can corrode hearing aid components faster. If you notice your hearing aids deteriorating quickly, discuss protective measures with our audiologist.

Signs Your Hearing Aid Needs Replacement

  • Frequent Repairs: If your hearing aid needs repairs more than once or twice a year, it may be more economical to replace it.
  • Declining Performance: Sounds seem less clear or the device struggles in situations where it previously worked well.
  • Physical Damage: Cracks, loose components, or worn battery contacts that cannot be repaired.
  • Changing Hearing Needs: Your hearing loss has progressed and your current device cannot provide adequate amplification.
  • Outdated Technology: Newer models offer significantly better features that could improve your quality of life.

How to Extend Your Hearing Aid's Life

  • Daily Cleaning: Wipe with a soft cloth every night before bed.
  • Dehumidifier: Store in a dehumidifier box or drying kit overnight, especially during summer.
  • Remove Before Water: Always remove hearing aids before showering, swimming, or washing your face.
  • Open Battery Door: When not in use, open the battery compartment to allow air circulation and extend battery life.
  • Regular Servicing: Visit your audiologist every 6 months for professional cleaning and check-up.
  • Handle with Care: Always handle over a soft surface in case you drop the device.
